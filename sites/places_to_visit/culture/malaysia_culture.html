Culture of Malaysia

Cuisine

Cucur Udang

This meal is a very unique Malaysian food. This Signature snacks of Malaysian made from a mixture of flour mixed with fresh prawns, sliced leaf chives, and various other spices.

Nasi Lemak

This food is very symbolic country Malaysia. With a mixture of rice mixed with coconut milk and screw pine leaves, resulting in a wet oily texture. Then served with eggs, sliced cucumber, kerrang, squid, and chicken.

Kolo Mee

This food is very easy to get in Malaysia. This food is food that comes from the Sarawak region, Malaysia. Much like ordinary noodles but this one on serve quite different. Usually Kolo Mee is served with pork.

Traditional Clothes

Malaysia does have many areas, so the customs in Malaysia are also numerous. Like traditional clothes, traditional Malaysian clothes is called as Melayu Clothes. The clothes used by Malaysians are quite unique, with a scarf ornament and equipped with a cap. Malaysian lady wearing headscarves and dresses.

Currency

The currency applicable in Malaysia is the ringgit currency. This one currency has a pretty good stability. Despite the ups and downs of the Malaysian currency due to the economic and governance conditions that exist in Malaysia. The unique thing on the ringgit currency is on the front of the money. At the front there is a Malaysian character who is a very respected person in Malaysia.

Language

The language in use in this country is Bahasa Melayu. Formerly a Malaysian-owned language called Bahasa Malaysia but substituted by the government as the new name which is called as Bahasa Melayu. This language is similar to English and almost 80% Malay language is similar to Bahasa Indonesia.

Religions

Malaysia only recognizes 5 different religions, namely Islam, Buddhism, Christianity, Hinduism, and Catholicism. Malaysians have a building that symbolizes their religion like a historic statue in Malaysia. And there is also the largest building that symbolizes the religion of Islam.
